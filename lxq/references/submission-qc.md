## Submission fact base

Record target journal/article type, central claim, visible evidence, evidence boundary, likely readership, and missing materials. Assess originality, scientific importance, technical soundness, interdisciplinary relevance, and readability only from supplied evidence. Do not predict editorial acceptance or claim definitive journal fit.

Apply requirements in this order: target-journal instructions and decision letter; publisher policies; repository/tool requirements; project facts; conservative local guidance. Verify current policy pages when details may have changed.

## Reviewer-response verification

- Preserve editor instructions and reviewer comments faithfully. Assign stable IDs such as `E.1`, `R1.1`, and `R2.1`.
- Classify each item by concern, severity, action, owner, evidence needed, manuscript location, and readiness.
- Map every claimed response to an actual analysis, experiment, citation, manuscript change, figure/table/supplement, or explicit placeholder.
- Never invent performed work, line numbers, panels, citations, reviewer identities, or manuscript changes.
- When disagreement is justified, acknowledge the concern and give a scientific or scope-based reason. Do not cite time, cost, or convenience as the primary scientific rationale.
- Treat reviewer misunderstanding as possible evidence that manuscript presentation needs repair.

## Data and code availability

Inventory every artifact supporting main and supplementary results: raw data, processed data, figure source data, statistical tables, software outputs, models, images, code, environments, materials, and protocols.

Classify each into one access route:

- `public-repository`
- `controlled-access`
- `within-paper-or-supplement`
- `reused-public-source`
- `third-party-restricted`
- `justified-request`
- `not-applicable`

Verify dataset-to-location mapping, stable identifiers, versions, licenses, access conditions, metadata, README information, and links between source data and figures. Do not invent DOIs, accessions, repositories, embargoes, ethics approvals, committees, or data-use terms. Flag vague “available upon request” language unless a specific restriction and review route are stated.

## Citation support

- Segment compound claims before checking citations.
- Grade candidates as `strong`, `partial`, `background`, `contradictory-or-limiting`, or `metadata-only`.
- Do not treat title similarity, citation count, or journal prestige as claim support.
- Prefer primary evidence for experimental claims; label reviews as context when appropriate.
- Check visible corrections, retractions, or expressions of concern and preserve DOI-level provenance.

## Package readiness

Use one of:

- `ready_to_use`: required artifacts present, reconciled, and verified.
- `conditional`: no blocker, but specified major actions remain.
- `needs_author_input`: completion depends on facts or decisions only the author/project owner can provide.
- `blocked`: identity, evidence, provenance, policy, or central validity prevents responsible completion.

Readiness is not editorial acceptance and does not certify clinical validity.
